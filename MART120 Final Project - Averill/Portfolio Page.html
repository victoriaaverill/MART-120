Victoria Averill MART120 Portfolio

Week 1

For week 1, I did my introduction video for MART120. I introduced myself as well as discussed my relationship with coding.

Week 2

For week 2, using AppLab on Code.org, I put together a drawing of my own to create a pattern with a geometric design. This helped me with understanding basic computer concepts and logical reasoning.

Week 3

For week 3, using Scratch, I created an interactive game. The arrow keys were programmed to move the dinosaur to the right of the screen, and then every time the mouse clicks on a balloon, the color of the balloon changes and the background is generated at random as well.

Week 4

Week 4 using Code.org again, I created another game that counted points as well as repeated movement between the figures. The spaceman had to collide with the robot to get points and the alien was an obstacle that would make the screen go black as "you lost." Every time the spaceman collides with the robot, the points would go up. The robot was programmed to run away from the spaceman at random, making it a challenge.

Week 5

In week five, I used Alice to create a small, briefly interactive scene. I places props and characters and then had speech bubbles and actions all timed to be qued based on the user's actions. The alien was the player's character and the other two figures reacted based on the player's actions.

Week 6

For week 6, working with HTML, I created 3 web pages that go back and forth to each other with "next/previous Page" buttons. The first page consists of 3 lists: hobbies, restaurants, and websites. Page 2 consists of a story with an image. Page 3 Is a contact information page.

Week 7

Working with CSS, I took my work from Week 6 and added color, backgrounds, and other cosmetic details to my webpages.

Week 8

For week 8, I incorporated JavaScript into my webpage with contact info by developing required fields. When a peice of the info was missing, after clicking submit, the page would highlight the missing fields in red. As a bonus, I participated in developing a math game which printed out percents and scores after answering 10 questions.

Week 9

Week 9 introduced p5.js which was used to develop a self portrait using geometric shapes.

Week 10

Mathh objects and basic operators were incorporated in the Week 9 project and used to make shapes of the self portrait move around the canvas.

Week 11

A canvas was created with a player and two obstacles of different sizes and colors. They moved randomly on the screen as the player is controlled with the "W" "A" "S" "D" keys. A third, non-moving obstacle appears where the mouse is clicked. Collisions were ignored and by using logical operators, "you win" is displayed once the player reached the exit on the bottom right of the canvas.

Week 12

Using week 11's project, Functions are used entirely.

Week 13

Week 13's project is the same project from week 11, however Arrays are used to create random shapes and random directions and movements for the shapes throughout.
